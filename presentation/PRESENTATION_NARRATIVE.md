### What 2,852 voices reveal about India's EV bus opportunity · TIVA AI 2026 · Group 1

**The one-line story:** *The people who buy electric buses and the people who ride them are having two different conversations — and the OEM that closes that gap wins the school and employee transport segments.*

## Act 1 — "We went and listened" (the setup)

**Chart:** `01_corpus_voices.png` — *Parents speak in fragments; institutions write books*

Open with scale and method, not tech: we assembled **2,852 assets across four voices** — 2,747 rider/parent comments from four metro subreddits, six institutional reports (ITDP, SIAM, CESL, UITP, UNCRD — 66,309 words), 13 news articles, and 80 YouTube videos with engagement metadata.

**Speaker line:** *"Notice the shape of this data. Parents produce thousands of 40-word fragments; institutions produce six 10,000-word reports. Any analysis that treats these the same will only hear the institutions. Our pipeline was built so the fragments count too."*

**Datapoints to say out loud:** 2,852 assets · 155k words · 4 voices · 2-year window (Jul 2024 – Jul 2026).

## Act 2 — "They're not having the same conversation" (the H1 divergence)

**Chart:** `02_salience_divergence.png` — *Institutions talk money; the safety conversation lives on the street*

The core finding. Using transparent, hand-auditable lexicons (blueprint §4 Level 7 salience indexes):

- Institutional reports mention **economics terms 2.26× per 100 words** but **safety only 0.04** — a 56:1 ratio toward money.
- Riders & parents flip the weighting: their safety-to-economics ratio is **~25× heavier** than the institutions'.
- **Green/ESG language is almost absent in rider voice (0.03/100 words).** The sustainability pitch that dominates OEM decks barely exists in the conversation of the people who board the bus.

**Chart:** `03_qrd_gap.png` — *Institutions argue in numbers; parents argue in stories*

Quantitative Reasoning Density (numerals + units per 100 words): **3.7 institutional vs 1.1 rider/parent — a 3.3× gap**. Buyers quantify; parents narrate.

**Speaker line:** *"A procurement officer reads a TCO table. A parent reads a story about a bus that almost toppled into a pothole. Both are deciding about the same bus. Marketing that answers only one of them loses the other."*

## Act 3 — "And the street conversation is anxious" (the emotional stakes)

**Chart:** `04_safety_sentiment.png` — *When safety comes up, positivity vanishes*

VADER sentiment over all 2,747 comments: **47% of safety-related comments are negative vs 28% of everything else**. Safety isn't just the most salient parent topic — it's the topic where sentiment collapses. This is the trust deficit an EV school bus product must repair before any economics argument lands.

**Chart:** `05_city_sentiment.png` — *Commuter mood is not uniform: Delhi grumbles, Hyderabad cheers*

Negative share ranges **19% (Hyderabad) → 33% (Delhi)**. Rider mood tracks local rollout experience — which means city selection for a pilot is not just an infrastructure decision, it's a sentiment decision.

**Chart:** `07_discourse_timeline.png` — *The conversation arrives in bursts — incidents and launches set the agenda*

Monthly volume is spiky, not smooth: accident threads and fleet-launch news create the big months. Two implications: (1) negative incidents dominate organic reach — one school-bus accident thread outdraws months of marketing; (2) launch windows are when the audience is actually listening.

**Speaker line:** *"You don't get to schedule the safety conversation. An incident schedules it for you. The strategic question is whether your brand shows up in that thread with a credible safety story or an ESG slogan."*

## Act 4 — "The market's own content proves the gap" (the H2 evidence)

**Chart:** `06_engagement_scatter.png` — *Reach doesn't buy engagement (ρ = −0.29) — and OEM channels engage least*

Across 77 videos: views and engagement rate are **negatively** rank-correlated, and **OEM channels post the lowest median engagement (1.0%) — half of what independent EV creators earn (2.0%)**. OEMs are buying reach but not resonance; the creators who talk like riders out-engage the brands 2:1.

**Speaker line:** *"The engagement data says the audience trusts people who sound like them. That's an argument for creator partnerships and testimonial content over polished launch films — and it's in the OEMs' own YouTube numbers."*

**Honesty note (pre-empt Q&A):** n = 77, Spearman association, engagement snapshot from the source sheet — we claim an association, not a predictive model.

## Act 5 — "And we built the machine to keep answering questions" (the pipeline proof)

**Chart:** `08_rag_tuning.png` — *Retrieval is not the bottleneck: 4 of 5 configs score a perfect MRR*

The corpus feeds a live RAG system: MiniLM embeddings → FAISS → BM25 hybrid with reciprocal-rank fusion → cross-encoder reranking → grounded generation with inline citations and a demonstrated refusal gate. The chunking grid hit **Hit@5 = 1.0 on every config** over 12 gold queries, under 17 ms per query.

**Speaker line:** *"Everything you've seen tonight is a query away. Ask the system 'what do parents fear about electric school buses' and it answers from these 2,852 documents — with citations — or refuses when the corpus can't support an answer. We demo the refusal too, because a system that can't say 'I don't know' can't be trusted in a boardroom."*

Optionally run live: `python rag_query.py "What concerns do parents raise about school bus safety?"`

## Close — the strategy in three sentences

1. **Lead school-segment positioning with safety, not sustainability** — parents weigh safety ~25× heavier than institutions do, green language is absent from rider discourse (0.03/100 words), and safety talk is where sentiment collapses (47% negative).
2. **Speak both languages:** TCO tables for the procurement office (QRD 3.7), trust narratives for the parent WhatsApp group (QRD 1.1) — same bus, two campaigns.
3. **Borrow credible voices:** independent creators out-engage OEM channels 2:1, and incident threads — not ad campaigns — set the discourse calendar.

## Q&A defense card (know these before presenting)

| Likely question | Answer |
|---|---|
| "Are your salience indexes validated?" | They're transparent dictionary metrics — every trigger word is listed in `make_charts.py` and hand-auditable. We report them as descriptive salience, not a psychometric instrument (blueprint §4 L7 caveat). |
| "Is VADER reliable on Indian English?" | Western-trained — it's in our bias register. We use it for *relative* comparisons (safety vs non-safety, city vs city) where miscalibration applies to both sides. |
| "Reddit isn't representative." | Correct, and we say so: urban, English-speaking, tech-adjacent — underrepresenting exactly the parents the school segment serves. That's why claims are scoped to discourse, not population attitudes. |
| "Is the timeline a real trend?" | Partly sampling: thread-level scraping means volume spikes reflect when big threads occurred. We present it as burst structure, not growth. |
| "Correlation or causation on engagement?" | Association only — Spearman rank, n = 77, no confound controls beyond segment split. We'd control channel size with a bigger sample. |
| "Why is MRR perfect — is the eval too easy?" | Fair — 12 gold queries authored by us against a 3k-chunk corpus; it demonstrates the harness works, and we note richer eval (blind queries, faithfulness scoring) as next step. |
| "Divergence proves adoption stalls?" | No — we quantify the divergence and argue its strategic implication. No adoption outcome variable exists per document (blueprint §2.3 note). |
